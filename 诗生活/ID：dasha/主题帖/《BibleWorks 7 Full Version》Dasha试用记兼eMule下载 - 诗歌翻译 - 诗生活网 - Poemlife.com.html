  The Book of Kells is one of the most famous books in the
history of the world and was completed in about 800 AD.
  The vellum (calfskin) manuscript containstranscriptions
of the four Gospels, lavishly illustrated and ornamented.
It is the most elaboratemanuscript of its kind to survive
from the early Middle Ages.
  The scribes and artists who created the Book were Columban
monks who lived in a monastery on the remote island of Iona,
off the west coast of Scotland. The monastery was founded
late in the sixth century by an Irish monk, St Colm Cille.  
  At the time the book was produced, Irish monks were
renowned throughout the rest of Europe for their work
as scribes and illustrators.
  The Book of Kells contains 680 pages (or 340 folios).

thirty folios, including some major decorated pages,
