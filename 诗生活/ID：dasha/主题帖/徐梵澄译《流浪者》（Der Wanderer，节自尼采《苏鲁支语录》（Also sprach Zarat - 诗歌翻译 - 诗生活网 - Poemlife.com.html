也来感受一下尼采的散文诗——从英译<p>尼采《流浪者》（Der Wanderer）</p>
<p>Thus spake Zarathustra to himself while ascending, comforting his heart with harsh maxims: for he was sore at heart as he had never been before. And when he had reached the top of the mountain-ridge, behold, there lay the other sea spread out before him: and he stood still and was long silent. The night, however, was cold at this height, and clear and starry.</p>
<p>当查拉图斯特拉登山时他这样对自己说，用粗糙的格言安慰自己的心：因为他感觉到自己的心从来没有如此创痛。当他来到山岭的最高处时，看啊，另一片大海在他的面前展开：他静静站在那里，长久无语。无论如何，夜晚在这个高度还是寒冷的，它是那样洁净，充满了星光。</p>
<p>Then, when it was about midnight, Zarathustra went his way over the ridge of the isle, that he might arrive early in the morning at the other coast; because there he meant to embark. For there was a good roadstead there, in which foreign ships also liked to anchor: those ships took many people with them, who wished to cross over from the Happy Isles. So when Zarathustra thus ascended the mountain, he thought on the way of his many solitary wanderings from youth onwards, and how many mountains and ridges and summits he had already climbed.</p>
<p>那时，当已经接近午夜，查拉图斯特拉在岛屿山脊的道路上向前走，他想清早到达另一个海岸，好在那儿上船。因为那儿有一个很好的停泊处，那些外国的船只都喜欢在那儿下锚。那些船上载着许多人，他们都希望离开幸福岛越洋而去。当查拉图斯特拉登上高山，他想起自己从年轻时到现在的许多孤独流浪的道路，啊，有多少山脉，岗岭和顶峰自己已经登临。</p>

<p>I am a wanderer and mountain-climber, said he to his heart, I love not the plains, and it seemeth I cannot long sit still.</p>

<p>我是一个流浪者，一个登山者，他对自己的心说，我不喜欢平原，而且似乎也不能够长久静坐。</p>

<p>And whatever may still overtake me as fate and experience--a wandering will be therein, and a mountain-climbing: in the end one experienceth only oneself.</p>

<p>无论如何，寂静都会像命运和经验一样追赶上我——一个流浪者和一个登山者会在那里，最终只有一个人能够理解他自己。</p>

<p>The time is now past when accidents could befall me; and what COULD now fall to my lot which would not already be mine own!</p>

<p>时代已经过去了，当那偶然能够降临于我时，现在除了我自己已经拥有的一切，还有什么能够降临给我的命运呢！</p>

<p>It returneth only, it cometh home to me at last--mine own Self, and such of it as hath been long abroad, and scattered among things and accidents.</p>

<p>这又转回来了，最终还是回到我——我自己，这久滞异国的人，这事物和偶然中的散居者。</p>

<p>And one thing more do I know: I stand now before my last summit, and before that which hath been longest reserved for me. Ah, my hardest path must I ascend! Ah, I have begun my lonesomest wandering!</p>

<p>还有一件事我是知道的：现在我站在这最后的峰顶前面，而在此之前，它已经为我许久地保留。啊，我要攀登那最艰难的道路！啊，我已经开始了我最孤寂的流浪！</p>
